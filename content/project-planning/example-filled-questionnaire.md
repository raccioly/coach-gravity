# 📋 Example: Filled Questionnaire — "Volunteer Hub"

> This is an example of a completed questionnaire for a real project. Use it as a reference for how to fill out your own.

---

## 🎯 Project Vision

**1. What is this project?**
A website for a nonprofit that connects volunteers with people who need help in our community. Volunteers sign up, clients request help, and we match them.

**2. Who is this for?**
Three groups: volunteers who want to help, clients who need services, and our small admin team who manages everything.

**3. What problem does it solve?**
Right now we manage everything in spreadsheets and email. It's slow, we lose track of requests, and volunteers don't know what's available. We need a single place for everything.

**4. What does success look like?**
Volunteers can browse available requests and sign up. Clients can submit requests online instead of calling. Our admin team can see everything in one dashboard.

---

## 🏗️ Scope & Features

**5. What are the must-have features?**
- Volunteer registration form
- Client request form
- Admin dashboard showing all volunteers and requests
- Matching system (assign a volunteer to a request)
- Email notifications when a match is made

**6. What are the nice-to-have features?**
- Volunteer hours tracking
- Client feedback after service
- Map showing service areas
- Monthly reports for our board

**7. Are there existing tools or services it needs to connect to?**
We'd like it to send emails (notifications when matched). No other integrations needed right now.

**8. How many pages or screens will it need?**
Maybe 8-10? Home page, about us, volunteer signup, client request, admin dashboard, volunteer list, request list, maybe a contact page.

---

## 👤 Users & Access

**9. Who can access it?**
The main site should be public (anyone can see it). But the admin dashboard needs login.

**10. Are there different user roles?**
Yes — admin (full access), coordinator (can match but not delete), and viewer (read-only for board members).

**11. How will people log in?**
Email and password for admin. Volunteers and clients don't need accounts — they just fill out forms.

---

## 🎨 Look & Feel

**12. What's the vibe?**
Warm, community-focused, professional but not corporate. Think "helpful neighbor" not "Fortune 500."

**13. Any websites you admire?**
I like how habitat.org looks — clean, warm, with good photos.

**14. Do you have a logo or brand colors?**
Yes — our logo is blue and gold. Blue: #2563EB, Gold: #EAB308.

**15. Light mode, dark mode, or both?**
Light mode only — our users are not tech-savvy and dark mode might confuse them.

---

## 📱 Platform

**16. Where will people use it?**
Both desktop and mobile. Many of our clients access things on their phones.

**17. Any specific browser requirements?**
No — just needs to work on Chrome and Safari.

---

## 🔧 Technical

**18. Do you have a domain name?**
Not yet, but we want one like `volunteerhub.org` or similar.

**19. Where should it be hosted?**
AWS — whatever you recommend.

**20. Do you already have any data?**
Yes — we have a spreadsheet with 200 volunteers and 150 past requests. I can export it as CSV.

**21. Do you need to store user data?**
Yes — volunteer names, emails, phone numbers, skills, availability. Client names and service requests.

---

## 📊 Business Context

**22. Is this for a business or personal use?**
Nonprofit organization. 501(c)(3).

**23. Will you charge users?**
No — completely free for everyone.

**24. What's your timeline?**
We'd love to launch in 4-6 weeks but there's no hard deadline. Quality over speed.

**25. What's your budget for hosting/services?**
Very limited. Free tier would be ideal. Up to $20/month is our max.

---

## 💡 Anything Else?

**26. Is there anything else we should know?**
Many of our clients speak Spanish, so bilingual support (English/Spanish) would be really important. Also, our admin team is not tech-savvy — the admin dashboard needs to be really simple and intuitive.
